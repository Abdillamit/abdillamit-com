My name is Abdyllamit. I'm from Baul — a village in the Leilek district of Batken region, in the far south of Kyrgyzstan. Mountains, silence, cows and sheep. Not the most obvious place for a programmer to come from.

## My brother and the first push

It all started with my brother Daniyar. He worked at ManchoDevs, an IT company in Bishkek, and one day told me about it. He talked about the team's professionalism, the projects they worked on, the culture. I listened and thought: this sounds like a different world.

When I was in 9th grade, we went to Bishkek together. I visited the ManchoDevs office. Something clicked.

## The office that changed everything

I expected to see just rooms with computers. Instead — people genuinely excited about what they were doing. Discussing problems, helping each other, building real products. There was an atmosphere hard to put into words — somewhere between focus and excitement.

I knew: this is what I want. Not just a job — a place where you grow every single day.

## Back to the village. Teaching myself.

When I returned home, the holidays ended — and a different process began. I decided to learn on my own.

Udemy, YouTube, tutorials in English I barely understood at the time. HTML first, then CSS, then JavaScript. Then React. Every day — a little, but every day.

I built mini-projects. Watched how others solved problems. Started LeetCode — not because anyone told me to, but because I wanted to understand how programmers think.

## LeetCode between pastures

I had normal village responsibilities — herding cows and sheep. A few hours a day, every day. I didn't give up studying for that. I just combined them: mornings or evenings — code. In between — livestock.

Some might call that strange. To me it was just practical. Everyone has different resources and time. The main thing is not to wait for perfect conditions.

## Where it led

I applied to Mancho School — an educational project by ManchoDevs. Got in. Started learning properly — with mentors, real tasks, real feedback. In 2022 I started working professionally.

Now I'm a software engineer at ManchoDevs. I work on finik.kg, slonexpress, mancho.school. Stack: TypeScript, React, Gatsby.js, Apollo GraphQL.

The path from Baul village to this took a few years. It was nonlinear and sometimes strange. But I wouldn't change it.

If you're somewhere far from everything and think IT isn't for you — maybe you're wrong.
